<section id="guidelines">
  <h2>Submission Guidelines</h2>
  <p>
    The <strong>Dubai International Academy Al Barsha Research Journal</strong> is committed to 
    maintaining high academic standards and providing a professional, student-driven 
    platform for early researchers. Authors must carefully follow the guidelines below 
    to ensure that their submission can be considered for peer review and publication.
  </p>
  <h3>1. Types of Accepted Submissions</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Original Research Articles</strong>: Present original findings in STEM, AI, Medicine, Social Sciences or Aerospace. 
        Minimum word count: <strong>500 words</strong>, though most articles are 1000–6000 words.</li>
    <li><strong>Review Articles</strong>: Provide a critical overview of existing work. 
        Minimum word count: <strong>800 words</strong>, typically 3000–8000 words.</li>
    <li><strong>Lab or experiment reports, proposals and Case Studies</strong>: Summaries of projects, experiments, 
        or engineering solutions relevant to our scope.</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>2. Manuscript Structure</h3>
  <p>We recommend the following structure:</p>
  <ol>
    <li><em>Title Page</em>: Title, author names, affiliations, and contact email.</li>
    <li><em>Abstract</em>: 150–250 words summarizing the work.</li>
    <li><em>Keywords</em>: 3–6 words or phrases.</li>
    <li><em>Introduction</em>: State the problem and context.</li>
    <li><em>Methods</em>: Explain research methodology or approach.</li>
    <li><em>Results</em>: Present findings clearly (figures and tables encouraged).</li>
    <li><em>Discussion</em>: Interpret results and compare with prior work.</li>
    <li><em>Conclusion</em>: Summarize contributions and suggest future work.</li>
    <li><em>References</em>: APA style consistently applied.</li>
    <li><em>In-text citations</em>: APA format using the "(name, year)" format.</li>
  </ol>

  <h3>3. Formatting Requirements</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Manuscripts must be in English.</li>
    <li>PDF submission only (upload via our form).</li>
    <li>Figures and tables must be clearly labeled and referenced in text.</li>
    <li>Equations should be typeset using proper math editors (not screenshots).</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>4. Ethical Considerations</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Submissions must be the authors’ original work — plagiarism is strictly prohibited.</li>
    <li>All co-authors must approve the final version before submission.</li>
    <li>Data integrity must be maintained; falsification or fabrication is grounds for rejection.</li>
    <li>Human or animal studies must comply with relevant ethical standards.</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>5. Peer Review and Revisions</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>All articles undergo blind peer review.</li>
    <li>Authors may receive reviewer comments requesting minor or major revisions.</li>
    <li>Revised manuscripts should be re-submitted with a point-by-point response.</li>
    <li>Decisions: Accepted, Minor Revision, Major Revision, or Rejected.</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>6. Dual Submission Policy</h3>
  <p>
    To empower students, we allow <strong>dual submissions</strong>. Authors can 
    submit their article to this journal while also submitting elsewhere. 
    We only request that authors inform us if their work is accepted by another venue, 
    so we can avoid duplicate publication.
  </p>

  <h3>7. Final Checklist</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>✓ Title and abstract are complete and polished.</li>
    <li>✓ Word count ≥ 500 (research) or ≥ 1000 (review).</li>
    <li>✓ References formatted consistently.</li>
    <li>✓ Figures/tables are included with captions.</li>
    <li>✓ PDF uploaded via the submission form.</li>
  </ul>
</section>
